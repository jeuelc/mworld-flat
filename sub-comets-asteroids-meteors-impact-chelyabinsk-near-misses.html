Near misses

Along with the impact of things hitting the Earth's atmosphere, there have been several near misses as asteroids, comets and meteoroids have passed very close to the Earth. Less than a day after the Chelyabinsk meteor blast in February 2013, a 30-metre asteroid (2012 DA14) passed within 30,000 kilometres of Earth.

That sounds like a long way off, but that's actually really close. It is less than one tenth of the distance from the Earth to the Moon.

In 1972, there was a closer shave when an asteroid skipped across the top of the atmosphere and flew back into space. It flew over Grand Tetons in Wyoming, where a quick-thinking visitor recorded the meteor's vapour trail in the atmosphere with a home-movie camera.
