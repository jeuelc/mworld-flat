Chelyabinsk

 

On 15 February 2013, a 10,000-tonne meteor entered the atmosphere near Chelyabinsk, Russia. Thousands of people saw this meteor and it was recorded by car dashboard cameras, security cameras and mobile phones.

The asteroid hit the top of the atmosphere travelling at almost 20 kilometres every second (that's 72,000 km per hour!). About 20 kilometres above the ground, the meteor exploded, releasing enormous energy. The explosion was so large that sound waves from the explosion, in the form of infrasound (which people cannot hear), were recorded around the world!

After the bright flash of the explosion, people went outside to see what had happened. Several minutes later, the shockwave reached the Earth's surface the shockwave travelled a lot slower than the speed of the meteor, it travelled at the speed of sound, while the flash of light travelled at the speed of light! The shockwave shattered windows, blew in doors and caused over 1000 injuries! Luckily the Chelyabinsk blast was not as powerful as the Tunguska event from a century earlier.
