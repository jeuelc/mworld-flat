Can you stop an asteroid impact? It's getting more possible every year.

You need to find and track all of the dangerous asteroids, and that isn't easy. Asteroids travel hundreds of millions of kilometres each year. Dangerous asteroids can be as little as tens of metres wide, so they aren't easy to spot when they are millions of kilometres away! They are especially hard to spot when they are coming from a direction near the Sun, as they need to be spotted in daylight that's why nobody spotted the defence meteorite.

Many astronomers are hunting for asteroids. Telescopes on the ground and satellites in space have hunted for asteroids for years. In the future, giant telescopes like the large synoptic survey telescope or 'LSST', will allow us to spot asteroids that are smaller and further from Earth. The LSST should start operating in the 2020s.

If you found an asteroid heading for Earth, what could you do? You could blow it up, but that might be a mistake, since it might just shatter the asteroid, sending smaller, possibly still dangerous pieces to Earth.

A better option may be to push the asteroid. You only need to push an asteroid a few centimetres when it is far away from the Earth to result in it missing the Earth by tens of thousands of meters. The push may be delivered gently using an electronic rocket physically nudging the asteroid, or using a nuclear bomb detonated above the asteroid's surface so that the force of the blast pushes it into a slightly different orbit.
