Sudan

On 6 October 2008, Richard Kowalski discovered a small asteroid in images taken with a telescope in Arizona. Named '2008 TC3', the asteroid was only 20 hours away from Earth! The asteroid was speeding across the night sky where would it hit?

Around the world astronomers got together to track the path of the asteroid. They worked out it would hit the atmosphere above the Sudanese desert in Africa.

Very few people saw the asteroid enter the Earth's atmosphere above the Sudanese desert on the morning of Tuesday 7 October. However, some airline pilots saw the flash from 1000 kilometres away. Weather satellites and spy satellites saw the asteroid explode tens of kilometres above the Earth! Although it weighed less than 100 tonnes and was only four metres across, it made a huge blast.

Six hundred meteorites, fragments of 2008 TC3, were later found scattered across the desert! Almost 100 tonnes of asteroid was reduced down to smoke and hundreds of fragments.
