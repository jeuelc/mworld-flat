Tunguska - What was that?

 

Asteroid and comet impacts didn't just happen millions of years ago. They can still happen today. In 1908, an asteroid or comet slammed into the Earth's atmosphere. The blast could be seen from 400 kilometres away, but not many people saw it because it happened over a remote part of Siberia. People who did see it struggled to describe what they had seen.

At the site, trees were flattened over many kilometres by an explosion as powerful as a nuclear bomb, that's the same as blowing up millions of tonnes of TNT!

Bizarrely, tree trunks stood upright near the centre of the blast. The asteroid or comet must have exploded kilometres above the trees, so trees directly beneath the explosion were stripped of their branches but their trunks weren't tipped over!
