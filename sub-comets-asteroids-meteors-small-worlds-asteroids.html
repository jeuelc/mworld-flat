You may know the eight planets and Pluto, but have you heard of Ceres, Vesta and Ida? These are asteroids, made from matter left over from the formation of the solar system.

Most of the asteroids found are in the Asteroid Belt, between Mars and Jupiter. The gravitational pull of Jupiter, 317 times the strength of that of the Earth, and the Sun (333,000 times that of the Earth), may have stopped the asteroids forming into a planet in this part of the solar system.

There are huge numbers of asteroids in the solar system. There may be more than a million asteroids bigger than one kilometre across! There are many smaller ones too but even if you added all the asteroids together, their mass and size would be less than that of our moon.
