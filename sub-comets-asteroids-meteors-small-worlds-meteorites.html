Meteorites range in size from micrometeorites that are measured in micrometres to meteorites that can be found up to a few metres in size and can weigh many tonnes. Some meteorites are made of stone. These 'stony meteorites' often contain lots of pebbles and grains (called chondrules) that were stuck together by gravity billions of years ago, when the solar system formed.

Some meteorites are made of metal, known as 'iron meteorites'! On Earth you make metal in a factory, but how do you forge metal in outer space? Well, some asteroids, like planets, had centres made of molten iron. Some of these asteroids were smashed into pieces during collisions with other asteroids, and the fragments of these shattered worlds sometimes fall to Earth or land on other planets, like Mars. The third type of meteorite known as 'stony-iron meteorites' are less common. They have a sub-group known as Pallasites which are particularly spectacular meteorites. Tap the card to see one!
