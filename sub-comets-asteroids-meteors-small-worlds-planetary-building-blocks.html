The solar system was made approximately 4.7 billion years ago. What did it look like back then? How were the planets made?

The big things like planets grew from many smaller things, like asteroids, meteorites and comets.

We can see parts of space where new stars are being born. These places have loads of gas, dust and ice. As stars are born, it looks as though they are surrounded by spinning disks of matter.

Within these disks, bits of dust and ice can stick together, making bigger and bigger objects. Things grow from millimetres in size, then to metres and then to kilometres. There are some spectacular collisions. Our moon even may have been made by a giant collision!

Some objects get so big that their gravitational pull means that they can attract more fragments than smaller ones can, dragging in more and more matter including gas. These become planets. But some objects never get to this stage and remain smaller. These leftovers are the comets, asteroids and meteors we see today.
