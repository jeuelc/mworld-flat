Could people ever visit asteroids? Absolutely.

Asteroids may be the first place inter-planetary astronauts visit. The Near Earth Objects asteroids come close enough to Earth that astronauts don't have to travel far. Asteroids have no atmosphere and weak gravity, so hovering and landing on their surface is easy. In fact, gravity is so weak that astronauts may need to nail or glue handles into the surface!

Some of the smallest asteroids are only metres across. One idea is that astronauts or a robotic spacecraft could catch the asteroid, put it in a bag, and drag it closer to Earth! Scientists could then study it in detail, without having to fly millions of kilometres across interplanetary space to get to it.
