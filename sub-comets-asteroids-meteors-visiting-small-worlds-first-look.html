In 1975 the two Viking spacecraft provided our very first look at small worlds. They didn't go to an asteroid or a comet, but took a close look at the tiny moons of Mars, Phobos and Deimos.

If you went to the rocky moons of Mars in your very own spaceship, you would see they have craters and mountains, like our moon. But unlike our moon, they are only tens of kilometres across and they are shaped like potatoes. On big planets, gravity pulls the planet into a circular shape, but on small worlds gravity is too weak and we get irregular lumps. That's why these small moons and most asteroids are irregularly shaped!

These small moons may be asteroids captured by Mars. However, they could also be debris from titanic collisions between asteroids and Mars in the distant past.
