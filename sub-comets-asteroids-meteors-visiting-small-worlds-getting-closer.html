Getting closer

Since the 1990s, we have been able to take closer looks at small worlds.

The Galileo spacecraft flew by the asteroids Gaspra in 1991 and Ida in 1993 on its voyage to Jupiter. Scientists were surprised to discover that Ida (31 km in length) has its own tiny moon orbiting it (now called Dactyl), just over a kilometre across.

Other spacecraft have taken a closer look as well. Deep Impact visited the comets Tempel 1 and Hartley 2, taking very detailed pictures. In 2005, Deep Impact fired a probe at Tempel 1. The impact blasted a cloud of ice, gas and dust into space! Scientists analysed the cloud to measure what the comet was made up of.

In 2003, the Japanese Hayabusa spacecraft was sent to bring back dust from an asteroid. After many technical hitches, scientists were nervous, but Hayabusa finally landed on the asteroid Itokawa and collected a sample. Seven years after launch, Hayabusa returned to Earth, landing in an Australian desert. A re-entry capsule safely made it to Earth but not the rest of spacecraft! It burnt up high above the Earth, as it was supposed to do.
