Can you imagine trying to find food in the dark? Well lots of sea animals do! They have developed special ways to find their food, from making their own light, to using noises to work out what is nearby and far away.

Sperm whales are one of those animals that have learnt to hunt in the dark, as they can dive to great depths of 3000 metres and can hold their breath for two hours!

Inside their heads they have a special organ called spermaceti that is full of an oily fluid. Its main function is to make very loud clicks or noises. Theses clicks are used for communication and to find their food. The sound can travel up to 10 kilometres! When the click hits some food, like a giant squid, the click bounces back to the whale. The closer the squid is to the whale the quicker the click bounces back. When the prey is very close the clicks change to a 'creak' which is very fast clicks and chomp they have found dinner!

What is an orbit?

Does gravity always pull objects together? It does, but this doesn't always result in objects colliding with each other. If objects are set in motion, then they can orbit one another. That means they circle around each other.

A satellite travels so fast horizontally that instead of crashing to Earth, the satellite ends up circling the Earth instead. The Planets in our Solar System have orbited the Sun for billions of years!
