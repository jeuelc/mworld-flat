Did you know that the ocean is home to the biggest creature on Earth the Blue Whale! The Blue Whale is bigger than even the biggest dinosaur! It grows to 33 metres, that's the length of two and a half buses and did you know the largest creature on Earth eats one of the smallest? Krill are tiny shrimp-like animals which grow to just two centimetres and hang out in swarms made up of millions of tiny krill. The Blue Whale can eat four tonnes of the krill each day!

But krill are also a food source for many other animals like seals, penguins, squid and fish. So although they are very small and sit near the bottom of the food chain, krill are extremely important. Imagine how many of them must be down there!

What is an orbit?

Does gravity always pull objects together? It does, but this doesn't always result in objects colliding with each other. If objects are set in motion, then they can orbit one another. That means they circle around each other.

A satellite travels so fast horizontally that instead of crashing to Earth, the satellite ends up circling the Earth instead. The Planets in our Solar System have orbited the Sun for billions of years!
