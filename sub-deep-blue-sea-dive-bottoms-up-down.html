Did you know that more people have travelled into space than have visited the deep ocean, called the abyss? Which would you rather explore? The pressure is so extreme in the deep that we have had to invent other ways to explore this amazing part of the ocean! Only three people have made it to the bottom of the Mariana Trench, two people in 1960 and a solo explorer in 2012. But by the time you read this, maybe someone else has started a journey to the deep, dark abyss!

The 'Deepsea Challenger' submersible has special equipment to collect sediment from the deep, a robotic claw and a 'slurp gun' for sucking up small animals! Manned exploration like this is very dangerous, so we also invented remote-control robots. One of these robots was 'Nereus', which was connected to the exploration boat at the surface by kilometres of tiny fibre-optic cable, as thin as a human hair! Nereus got 10, 902 metres down and collected sediment samples and video of the extreme deep, dark place.
