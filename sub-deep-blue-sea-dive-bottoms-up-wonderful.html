Creatures of the deep can be pretty extreme looking characters. Since the pressure in the deep is 100 times that at the surface and there is no light to find dinner, these animals have evolved some unique features. Perhaps one of the scariest looking animals is the fangtooth fish, the deepest fish ever discovered. The fangtooth has a huge head and massive fangs for teeth but is only 16 centimetres long! That's not a lot longer than you hand. Its fangs are so long that when it closes its mouth the fangs fit into special pockets inside its head that sit either side of its brain!

Some fish in the deep can even make their own light. This is known as bioluminescence. The lanternfish has special organs on its head, belly and tail, called photophores and the chemical reactions inside the photophores give off light! The light attracts small fish for the lanternfish to eat, now that's a very clever hunter!

What is an orbit?

Does gravity always pull objects together? It does, but this doesn't always result in objects colliding with each other. If objects are set in motion, then they can orbit one another. That means they circle around each other.

A satellite travels so fast horizontally that instead of crashing to Earth, the satellite ends up circling the Earth instead. The Planets in our Solar System have orbited the Sun for billions of years!
