Coral reefs are one of the most productive places on Earth and are home to thousands of animals! Coral reefs can only survive in shallow warm water where sunlight can get through. The Great Barrier Reef in Australia is the largest living structure on Earth and is made up of 2,900 individual reefs. It covers over 2,600 kilometres and is so big it can been seen from space!

Did you know that coral is actually an animal? It belongs to the same family as jelly fish! Baby coral is only a few millimetres big and called a polyp. Millions of these polyps attach to a surface and form large colonies. Most importantly, corals release calcium carbonate which is like the skeleton of coral reefs.

The amazing colour that you see in corals is caused by tiny plants (algae) living inside. The algae use the sunlight to produce sugars and oxygen that the coral needs to survive, from a process called photosynthesis, a bit like what trees do in the forest! When the coral has no algal guests, it turns white and dies. This is known as coral bleaching.
