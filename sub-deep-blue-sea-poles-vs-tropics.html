Pole versus the Tropics

The world's oceans are influenced not just by depth of the water and sunlight, they are also influenced by how far they are from the Equator. The equator is a line which represents 0° latitude and divides the Northern and Southern hemispheres. Marine environments near the North and South Pole are very different to those in the tropics, near the Equator and much colder for swimming too! You would need a very thick wetsuit if you went swimming near the North or South Pole, and a really hot bath afterwards!

There are also different environments in the regions between the Equator and the Poles. We call these the temperate zones. There are also lots of differences between the North and South Poles themselves too. For example, there are no polar bears in the South, and no penguins in the North except at zoos.
