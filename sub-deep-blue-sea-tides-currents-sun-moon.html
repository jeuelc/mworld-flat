The great battle between the moon and the sun happens every day! It's the gravitational pull of the sun and moon that create the tides. The huge gravitational pull of the moon literally pulls the oceans towards it. But the sun is so much bigger than the moon, how come it doesn't have the same pulling effect on the ocean? Well that's because the sun is 400 times further away!

When the sun and moon line up or are opposite to each other, the tides are really big, these are called spring tides. Most places on earth have two high and low tides a day but other places, like Antarctica, only have onewhy do you think that might be?

What is an orbit?

Does gravity always pull objects together? It does, but this doesn't always result in objects colliding with each other. If objects are set in motion, then they can orbit one another. That means they circle around each other.

A satellite travels so fast horizontally that instead of crashing to Earth, the satellite ends up circling the Earth instead. The Planets in our Solar System have orbited the Sun for billions of years!
