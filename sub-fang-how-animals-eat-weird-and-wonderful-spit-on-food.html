Eating animals and plants is good if you can get them, but sometimes it's easier to eat something closer to home - dirt. While it doesn't look like much, dirt and mud have some nutrients in them, and there are many animals that spend their lives eating dirt. But dirt is not great food. You have to eat an awful lot to survive - sometimes 200 times your own body weight every day! For example, earthworms dig by eating the dirt in front of them and making tunnels they wriggle through.

For animals living in the sea, there's also food floating in the water, just tiny little specks of it. If you can somehow catch it, then you can eat it. So these animals have mini nets to catch the floating food that they eat. Some of the biggest animals that have ever existed live on some of the smallest!
