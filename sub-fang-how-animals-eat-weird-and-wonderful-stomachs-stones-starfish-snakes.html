Never eat anything bigger than your head did your mum ever tell you that? Certainly not good for us, but there are some animals that can do just that. Large snakes like boa constrictors can fit food into their mouth that's bigger than their heads by dislocating the bones in their skull and stretching their head around the food - sounds painful! The food, such as a whole sheep, then must fit in the snake's stomach, which is also extremely stretchy.

Other animals have strange stomachs - many birds and reptiles like crocodiles swallow stones. The stones in a chicken's stomach can help them grind up food because chickens like all birds, don't have teeth.

One last stomach story: starfish can push their stomach out of their mouth and use it to digest fish! The stomach releases enzymes to break down the fish before it swallows it in pieces.
