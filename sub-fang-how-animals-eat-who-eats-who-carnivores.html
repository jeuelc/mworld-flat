Eating animals can be good for you - after all, they contain all the same nutrients as your own body. Like plants, animals don't like to be eaten, but they are able to run, fight and hide to get away! If you want to be a carnivore, you have to find ways of hunting and chasing to get your dinner.

So carnivores usually have big, sharp teeth to help them catch food and tear it apart before they swallow it. In comparison to wombats, lions chew up and down with their bladed teeth, check out the video to see more! Think about yourself when you eat a bit of steak or a carrot. Which one do you chew more before swallowing? How long does your pet rabbit to feed in a day and compare that to how long your pet cat or dog takes to eat their meal? Cats and dogs have claws and sharp teeth to catch little animals like mice.

Animal tissue is quite easy to digest, though, because the cells are not surrounded by a wall like they are in plants.
