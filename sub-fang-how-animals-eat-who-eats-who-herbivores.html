If you walk outside now, odds are that the first food you'll see are plants - grass, maybe trees - and lots of it. Plants may seem great as food because they can't run away and it always looks like there's a lot around. However, they're not always as good for you as you might think.

There are lots of different parts of plants that herbivores might eat. While most plant parts that you see are the things like leaves and branches, other good plant foods are nectar, flowers, sap and even roots - if you are a mole or a beetle. Leaves and grass do have a lot of nutrients in them, but it's very hard to get them out because each cell is surrounded by a little wall. You don't get much out of eating lettuce unless you use really complex teeth to chew it up, which is what cows and horses do. If you squash a leaf from a bush between your fingers, green fluid comes out, showing it's quite easy to break it. Squashing grass or a gum leaf from a Eucalyptus tree, however, is much harder.

Many herbivores also have 'good' bacteria in their guts that help them break down the food, like wombats. Wombats chew side to side grinding their teeth, this also helps to break down the grass. Nectar is much easier to eat, but flowers are harder to find and you have to be very small, like a bee.
