Symbionts and Parasites

 
click here click here

Catching your own food can be hard work, so there are animals that form partnerships with others one supplies the food, the other makes the home and provides protection. For example, little plant-like algae can live inside the bodies of corals and sponges that live in the Great Barrier Reef in Australia. They get along very well, and each couldn't survive without the other - we call these 'symbionts'.

But sometimes animals steal food. Parasites can live inside another animal's body or stomach, taking food from its host! Of course the host doesn't like this and will try to defend itself, but parasites are experts at getting away with it. A really successful parasite doesn't take too much food because if the host dies so might the parasite!
