Which would you rather be bitten by - a crocodile or an echidna? No question you'd much prefer the echidna, assuming you could avoid its spines.

If an animal is trying to catch dinner, you have to be able to grab and hold onto it - the larger the prey, the harder you need to bite. Scientists recently measured how hard crocodiles can bite, and it seems they're the strongest of any animal ever measured! Crocodiles eat large animals that will try to escape, so a strong bite is very important.

Other animals may have equally strong bites, such as sharks, but nobody has volunteered to let a shark bite them!
