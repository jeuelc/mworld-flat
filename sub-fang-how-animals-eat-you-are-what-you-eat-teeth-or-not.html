Teeth or not...

There are lots of foods you can just pick up and swallow, but most need to be broken down into smaller pieces. That's what teeth are great for - you can bite off a leaf or a chunk of meat with your front teeth. Then with your back teeth you can chew it up really finely before you swallow.

Meat doesn't need too much chewing and is easily digested. So that's why dogs and cats have very simple teeth.

But leaves are a different story - animals need to chop them up finely, so they have teeth with lots of little blades over them. You find these teeth in sheep, cows and horses.

Some animals don't have teeth at all! If they are big enough they use their tongues to get insects and squash them. This is what echidnas do. The biggest animal of all, the blue whale, also doesn't have teeth. Instead it has 'baleen', which is like a net made of hair to trap little water creatures.
