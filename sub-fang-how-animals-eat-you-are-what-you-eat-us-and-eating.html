Chicken or beef? Italian or Chinese? These days we have such a huge choice in what to eat it's hard to imagine what our ancestors ate.

Our closest living relatives, chimps and gorillas, are largely vegetarian, but sometime during human evolution two important changes happened: we began to eat some meat, and we started cooking food. Since meat is higher in energy and protein, eating meat means that our ancestors could spend more time in other activities. Cooking is also important as it makes food more easily digested, particularly vegetables, and in fact makes some food edible that otherwise would be poisonous.

Although we have evolved to eat some meat, people today often eat too much fat and processed foods, which can cause huge health problems like obesity. Also, it is generally better for the environment to eat more plants, as it takes a lot less water and land.
