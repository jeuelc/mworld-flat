Suppose your friend is wearing a red jumper. Why does the jumper appear red when you look at it? Well, you have already learned that white light is a mixture of all of the colours of the rainbow. When white light shines on the jumper, only the red light bounces off the jumper. This bounced-off red light can then travel into your eye. This is why the jumper looks red! ?

Suppose, instead, that you were in a room filled with green light. If you were talking to your friend with the red jumper, it would look black! This is because the green light will not bounce off a red jumper. Since no light is bouncing off the jumper in this case, it will look black!
