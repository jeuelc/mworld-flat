Bioluminescence

 

There are some animals that can make their own light! Some of the fish that live in the very deep ocean can make their own light. The deep-sea anglerfish, for example, has a blob on the end of a stalk on its head which glows in the dark! It uses this to attract prey , which it then catches and eats!

Glow worms are another example of living things that can make their own light. While they are called "worms", actually they are insects!

There are also very tiny floating plants, called algae, which glow in the dark! Sometimes these are found in the ocean. When a boat passes through a part of the ocean, the algae in the water light up! The same thing happens when people swim through the algae, or when waves containing the algae crash on the sea shore, creating a spectacular light show for surfers and onlookers!
