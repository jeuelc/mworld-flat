Refraction and Rainbows

 

Suppose you put a pencil into a glass of water. It will look like it has been broken! . This is because light bends when it passes from water into the air. This bent light makes the pencil look broken!

The fact that light bends when it passes from water to air, or from air to glass, is called 'refraction'. Refraction can also be used in a prism, which is a triangle-shaped piece of glass. White light enters the prism. As you know, white light is made up of all the colours of the rainbow. Since the glass in the prism bends the different colours by different amounts, the light that comes out of the prism has all the colours of the rainbow!
