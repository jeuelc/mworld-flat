Pirates versus global warming

 

Scientists find out how the world works by gathering evidence, then using their brains to answer a question based on this evidence.

We must be careful with how we interpret data. For example, suppose someone told you the number of pirates in the world was going down, at the same time that average temperatures were going up ('"global warming'"). Does this mean that global warming is due to the fact that there are fewer pirates now than there used to be?

This example makes it clear that scientists need to be careful how they draw conclusions from their evidence. They must be careful not to draw a wrong conclusion, such as the absence of pirates causing global warming!

We are all made of atoms, and the different kinds of atoms are called elements. Oxygen, hydrogen, carbon, iron and gold are all elements.

In our daily lives, elements remain unchanged. You cannot easily turn hydrogen into helium or carbon into oxygen.

But in the super hot and super dense core of a star, hydrogen atoms are fused together to become helium atoms. This is nuclear fusion, and this process powers all stars including our Sun.
