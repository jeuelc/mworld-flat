Anyone can do science!

Many people volunteer their time for free and make a real contribution to science. They can help gather or sort information to be analysed by professional researchers. This is often called crowd-sourcing science because a crowd of people (just like you and me) all do a little bit, working towards a common goal, to discover something new or to make the world a better place.

People can also volunteer their computers! They do this by installing computer programs that can process scientific evidence during the free time on their computer when they are not using it themselves.

Also, they can help with studying scientific evidence using their own brains! The Zooniverse program is a good example (see www.zooniverse.org). Here, hundreds of thousands of people work together on large scientific projects. These projects are in lots of areas of science. People are asked to do things like, identify ocean animals in images of the sea floor, categorise whale calls to understand what the whales are saying, or classify galaxies by their shape.
