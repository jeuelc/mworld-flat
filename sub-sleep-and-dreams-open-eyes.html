Can't keep your eyes open?

 

Ever noticed that the longer you stay awake, the sleepier and sleepier you get until you can't keep your eyes open any more?

That's your homeostatic drive pushing you towards sleep. It is kind of like a pressure building inside of you. The pressure to sleep starts to build from the minute you wake up. It builds more and more the longer you are awake, until the body can't take it any more and you go to sleep. Even if you try and force yourself to try and stay awake, eventually the pressure becomes too much and you have to fall asleep.

Once asleep, the pressure releases and you can start all over again! The homeostatic drive works closely with your body clock; it is timed so that the point of highest pressure is also the point where your body clock tells you to sleep.
