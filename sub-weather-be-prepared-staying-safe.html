Staying safe

 

When living in an area that can have extreme weather, some people put together an 'action plan' so they know exactly what to do in an emergency. Most places have special emergency management people who are there to help. You can search for yours on the internet.

Some of the small things that people do to stay safe during very hot weather is drink lots of water and stay in cool, indoor areas with air conditioning or a fan.

IIn a flood, never walk, bike or drive through floodwater. Even fast-moving water below your knees can knock you off your feet! And you never know what is lurking under the surface.

Being caught in a place with strong winds can be dangerous. People stay out of harm's way as best they can by staying indoors, and in really strong winds, they keep away from windows.

What is an orbit?

Does gravity always pull objects together? It does, but this doesn't always result in objects colliding with each other. If objects are set in motion, then they can orbit one another. That means they circle around each other.

A satellite travels so fast horizontally that instead of crashing to Earth, the satellite ends up circling the Earth instead. The Planets in our Solar System have orbited the Sun for billions of years!
