Translating a weather map

 

To help forecast the weather, you look at a weather map, which is like a normal map but with extra lines and symbols on top. Here's how you work out what it means!

The lines are called 'isobars' and they show the atmospheric pressure, which is the weight of the air pressing down on us from above and can tell you a lot about the weather.

If where you live is under high pressure, the weather will be fine with no rain, little cloud and a light breeze. Alternatively, if where you live is under low pressure, you get thick clouds, rain and fast winds!

The symbols on weather maps show 'weather fronts', which are a type of weather system. There are warm fronts, and cold fronts. When a cold front passes, the weather can change from being warm to cold very quickly. If a warm front passes, the weather changes from cold to warm. This change is so big that you can go to school in a t-shirt and come home in a winter coat!
