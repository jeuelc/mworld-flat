You can tell the future!

Knowing what the weather will be like in the coming days is incredibly useful. It might be sunny in the morning, but perhaps it will rain in the afternoon. Who knows!? Meteorologists, that's who. They are the people who predict what will happen to the weather in the hours and days ahead, and they give you weather forecasts telling you this information.

Meteorologists look at the weather and take measurements using many types of weather instruments like thermometers, which measure temperature. As most weather happens above us, meteorologists put these instruments on big balloons that can travel 30 kilometres up in the sky much further than you can see! They also take measurements using pictures from satellites that look at us from space.

The measurements are given to a giant computer, which tells the meteorologist what the weather will probably be like up to one week in the future.
