Under pressure

 

The air in our atmosphere is one big soup of gases. These gases are swirling around us all the time. They ride on the wind and are up in the sky as far as you can see.

Even though they are invisible and too small to see, gases are made up of tiny things called molecules, which are very light, making them float in the sky. Even though each molecule is very light, there are HEAPS of them too many to count! All these molecules together are heavy and so they push down on us from above. We call this 'atmospheric pressure'.

But because the air swirls around us, the weight of the air above us changes all the time as the air moves, changing the pressure. And as the pressure changes, the weather changes with it!

What is an orbit?

Does gravity always pull objects together? It does, but this doesn't always result in objects colliding with each other. If objects are set in motion, then they can orbit one another. That means they circle around each other.

A satellite travels so fast horizontally that instead of crashing to Earth, the satellite ends up circling the Earth instead. The Planets in our Solar System have orbited the Sun for billions of years!
