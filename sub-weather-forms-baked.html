The weather systems that cause heatwaves are called blocking high-pressure systems because they act like big roadblocks, redirecting all the other weather systems, just like a roadblock redirects traffic!

Together, hot and dry weather can bake the landscape. When grasses, shrubs and trees dry out they make excellent fuel for a fire. With just one spark, a wildfire, also called a bush or forest fire, can burn through large areas of land in just hours. When you add strong winds, the resulting fire can be almost explosive!

What is an orbit?

Does gravity always pull objects together? It does, but this doesn't always result in objects colliding with each other. If objects are set in motion, then they can orbit one another. That means they circle around each other.

A satellite travels so fast horizontally that instead of crashing to Earth, the satellite ends up circling the Earth instead. The Planets in our Solar System have orbited the Sun for billions of years!
