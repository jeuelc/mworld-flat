When there is much less rain than normal we get a drought, which means that water is in short supply.

Lakes and rivers begin to dry up and plants, which need water to survive, can wilt or die. It can also mean there is less water available for people to use. So, in a drought we have to be careful not to use too much water.

In some places a lack of water means that there is a lot of dry dust. Sometimes, a weather system will come along which has strong winds, like a cold front. Then, like a giant fan, these winds whip the dust into a storm called a dust storm or a sand storm. /p>

Some places that are always dry, like deserts, have a special name for these storms; they call them 'haboobs'.
