A thunderstorm is born when warm and humid air bubbles up through the sky to form big, dark clouds.

The middle of the thunderstorm is high in the sky where the air is below -10°C, making the water drops in the clouds freeze.

Like bubbles in a boiling pot, the air in a thunderstorm moves the frozen water droplets around where they crash together to form hail. The hailstones will fall to the ground when they are heavy enough but they often melt back into rain on the way.

As the hailstones bounce around they create an electrical charge, which can make electricity. When the thunderstorm is fully charged, like a battery, it creates electricity as lightning! Even though it's so quick, a lightning flash is super-powerful. It will heat the air around it up to five times hotter than the surface of the sun! This sudden heat makes a BANG, which you hear as thunder.

What is an orbit?

Does gravity always pull objects together? It does, but this doesn't always result in objects colliding with each other. If objects are set in motion, then they can orbit one another. That means they circle around each other.

A satellite travels so fast horizontally that instead of crashing to Earth, the satellite ends up circling the Earth instead. The Planets in our Solar System have orbited the Sun for billions of years!
