Winter storms

 

Winter storms can bring very nasty, cold weather.

Winter storms happen when ice-cold air from the poles crashes into warmer air from the tropics. When these two combine, they can make strong winds and because some of the air is so cold, heavy snow as well. When strong winds and heavy snow combine they create a blizzard.

In some special cases, as the snow falls it melts into rain as it passes through a blanket of warm air sitting just above the ground. But the temperature is still below freezing at the ground, causing the rain to freeze instantly onto anything it touches!

So what happens then? Well, this creates layers of ice that are sometimes centimetres thick, turning a winter storm into an ice storm. Did you know that less than three centimetres of ice will add enough extra weight to break large tree branches and make power lines sag to the ground?

What is an orbit?

Does gravity always pull objects together? It does, but this doesn't always result in objects colliding with each other. If objects are set in motion, then they can orbit one another. That means they circle around each other.

A satellite travels so fast horizontally that instead of crashing to Earth, the satellite ends up circling the Earth instead. The Planets in our Solar System have orbited the Sun for billions of years!
